# Product

## Register

product

## Users

B2B procurement specialists and engineers sourcing heavy machinery parts (ECM modules, hydraulic systems, track motors, excavator components). They are domain experts working under time pressure — they know exactly what part number they need and want to find, verify, and purchase it without friction. Sessions are task-oriented: search → confirm spec → add to cart → checkout. Secondary users: sellers managing product listings, admins managing the platform.

## Product Purpose

SolydShop is an industrial B2B e-commerce marketplace for heavy machinery parts. It replaces slow manual procurement (phone calls, PDF catalogs, Alibaba searches) with a fast, spec-forward digital buying experience. Success looks like: a procurement specialist finds the right part by SKU in under 60 seconds, confirms specs without leaving the page, and completes checkout with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Bold · Efficient · Established

The tone is direct and expert — authoritative without being aggressive. No lifestyle language, no consumer-brand warmth. Copy reads like a trusted technical supplier who respects the buyer's time and knowledge. Confidence without arrogance. The visual weight should convey scale and reliability; buyers are making large, consequential purchases.

## Anti-references

- **Consumer retail (Amazon/Shopify):** No lifestyle imagery, no promotional badges ("🔥 HOT DEAL"), no rainbow accent colors, no oversized hero banners, no "You might also like" carousels.
- **Cluttered catalog chaos:** Not Alibaba/AliExpress density — no competing call-to-actions, no flashing price counters, no excessive promotions per card.
- **SaaS startup aesthetic:** No rounded gradient cards, hero metric layouts, glassmorphism, or anything that looks like a B2B software dashboard rather than a parts marketplace.
- **Grungy industrial cliché:** No gear/bolt imagery, no dark textures meant to evoke a workshop floor, no PDF-catalogue UI. The brand is industrial in its values (precision, durability) — not in its visuals.
- **Fashion / lifestyle store:** Not Shopify or Etsy. No editorial whitespace, no lifestyle photography grids, no luxury minimalism.

## Design Principles

1. **Spec first.** Technical details (SKU, part number, specs, stock status) are primary content, not supporting metadata. Put them where the eye lands first.
2. **Respect the expert.** Buyers know their domain. Skip tutorials, minimize tooltips, never condescend. The UI gets out of the way.
3. **Speed is the feature.** Every extra click or scroll before checkout is a failure. Filter → find → buy is the critical path; everything else is secondary.
4. **Density without clutter.** Show more product per viewport than consumer sites. Data density signals B2B credibility. But clutter signals chaos — use structure, not space, to separate.
5. **Industrial restraint.** Dark theme, muted palette, selective use of the brand accent. Color earns its place by signaling status or action — never for decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG AA (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text and UI components)
- Keyboard navigation required for all interactive elements (filters, cart, checkout)
- Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all transitions
- No color-only status signaling: stock status must use text label + color, not color alone
